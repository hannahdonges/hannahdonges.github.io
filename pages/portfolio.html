PORTFOLIO

Visual Design For several years, I've enjoyed making art through electronic design methods including graphic design (check out my logo - it forms all the letters of my first and last names) and front-end web design. My graphic design style is often intended to be humourous - often taking the form of intentionally bad photo editing or meme making. In recent years, I've also done a bit of basic front-end web design and development, mostly just for fun, as well as uni courses such as DESN2006 and COMP1710. In doing so, I have used languages including HTML, CSS, and Javascript.
Painting When I began painting, I started out with water colours on cotton paper as I loved the blending effects that can be created when working with water. After doing this for a while, I expanded into acrylics on canvas due to their more forgiving nature and ability to layer. In many of my paintings, I also like to work with silhouettes as they create a great layered effect and are relatively easy to create compared to pictures with fine details (which my impatience is not yet equipped to deal with).

Embroidery This is my most recent hobby which I have taken up. I was first inspired to start embroidery after scrolling through Instagram and seeing a few needle-work artists pop up on my feed. Currently, I'm working on creating a range of embroidered Canberra icons including the yellow bus stops, Belco owl statue, and soon Telstra Tower and Parliament House.

Watch my simple tutorial video to learn some basics of embroidery!
Metal work + 3D printing Through a few uni courses, I've been fortunate enough to get the chance to do some aluminium sand casting and various 3D printed pieces. For a Manufacturing Technologies assignment, I was tasked to design and create a handheld water pump. This involved numerous manufacturing processes to produce the finished piece - including casting, Metal finishing techniques, 3D printing, and some basic woodwork.

Woodwork Woodwork was probably my earliest hobby - from afternoons as a kid spent mucking around making little trinkets in my father's workshop, through to furniture making courses in highschool. Some of my better pieces include a sewing box with accordian-folding shelves, and a small coffee table complete with turned legs. Unfortunately, since moving interstate for uni, I haven't been able to do as much woodwork as I'd like to, but hope to do some more in the future.
Candle Making During the Great Lockdown of 2020, I was looking for something new and fun to do, which led me to try out candle making. After ordering a kit online, I enjoyed experimenting and refining my technique, and testing out all the scents I could get my hands on. Overall, the process was surprisingly easy and very cost effective compared to buying candles. Turns out 2kg of wax makes way too many candles for one person alone to use, so my family received a lot of great smelling, hand-poured soy wax candles for Christmas!
